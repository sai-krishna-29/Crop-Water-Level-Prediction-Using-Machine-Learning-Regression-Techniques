{% if crop_type == 'BANANA' %}

Crop Water Rotation Recommendations for Banana

First Two Weeks:

  • Provide frequent, shallow irrigation to establish young plants and encourage root growth.
  • Ensure soil remains consistently moist but not waterlogged to support seedling emergence.
  • Apply mulch around the base of banana plants to retain soil moisture and suppress weed growth.

Next Two Weeks:

  • Gradually reduce irrigation frequency but maintain soil moisture to support early growth.
  • Monitor soil moisture levels regularly and adjust irrigation as needed to prevent water stress.
  • Avoid overwatering to prevent waterlogging and root rot diseases.

Second Month:

  • Increase irrigation to meet the higher water demand during active vegetative growth.
  • Maintain consistent soil moisture during this stage to promote healthy leaf development.
  • Apply balanced fertilizer to support vigorous growth and development of banana plants.

Third Month:

  • Maintain consistent soil moisture during flowering and fruit development stages to ensure optimal yield.
  • Provide supplementary irrigation during dry periods to prevent water stress and fruit abortion.
  • Monitor weather conditions and adjust irrigation frequency accordingly to prevent waterlogging or drought stress.

Next 3 Months:

  • Adjust irrigation based on weather conditions and crop water needs to sustain fruit development and ripening.
  • Gradually reduce irrigation frequency as fruits mature and approach harvest to concentrate sugars and enhance flavor.
  • Harvest bananas when fully mature, and reduce irrigation to allow plants to enter dormancy before the next growth cycle.

Crop Water Rotation Recommendations for Beans

First Two Weeks:

  • Keep soil consistently moist to promote germination and emergence of bean seeds.
  • Provide light irrigation to prevent seedling washout and ensure uniform emergence.
  • Monitor soil moisture levels closely and adjust irrigation as needed to maintain optimal conditions.

Next Two Weeks:

  • Gradually reduce irrigation frequency but increase water volume to support root development.
  • Ensure soil remains evenly moist to prevent stress and encourage healthy growth.
  • Apply a balanced fertilizer to provide essential nutrients for vigorous vegetative growth.

Second Month:

  • Maintain moderate soil moisture levels to support flowering and pod formation.
  • Monitor plant health closely and adjust irrigation based on weather conditions and soil moisture levels.
  • Avoid overwatering to prevent waterlogging and root diseases such as root rot.

Third Month:

  • Increase irrigation during pod filling stage to prevent water stress and ensure plump, well-filled pods.
  • Apply a side dressing of nitrogen fertilizer to promote pod development and increase yield.
  • Monitor soil moisture closely and adjust irrigation to match crop water needs as plants approach maturity.

Next 3 Months:

  • Gradually reduce irrigation frequency as bean plants mature and approach harvest, allowing soil to dry slightly between waterings.
  • Harvest beans promptly when pods reach optimal size and maturity to prevent overripening and seed deterioration.
  • After harvest, reduce irrigation to allow soil to dry out and prepare for the next crop rotation or fallow period.

Crop Water Rotation Recommendations for Cabbage

First Two Weeks:

  • Keep soil consistently moist to promote uniform germination and establishment of cabbage seedlings.
  • Water newly transplanted seedlings immediately after planting to reduce transplant shock.
  • Apply a layer of mulch around cabbage plants to retain soil moisture and suppress weed growth.

Next Two Weeks:

  • Provide consistent irrigation to support early leaf growth and development.
  • Monitor soil moisture levels closely and adjust irrigation as needed to prevent water stress.
  • Avoid overhead irrigation to minimize foliar diseases and promote healthy growth.

Second Month:

  • Increase irrigation frequency during head formation stage to ensure rapid growth and prevent bolting.
  • Maintain even soil moisture to prevent cracking and splitting of cabbage heads.
  • Apply a balanced fertilizer to provide essential nutrients for healthy growth and head development.

Third Month:

  • Reduce irrigation slightly to prevent excess moisture buildup and minimize risk of disease.
  • Monitor cabbage heads closely for signs of maturity and harvest promptly when heads reach desired size and firmness.
  • After harvest, incorporate organic matter into the soil to improve soil structure and fertility for the next crop.

Next 3 Months:

  • Rotate cabbage with other crops to prevent buildup of pests and diseases specific to brassicas.
  • Monitor soil moisture levels regularly and irrigate as needed to support growth of succeeding crops.
  • Implement cover cropping or green manure practices to replenish soil nutrients and improve soil health.

Crop Water Rotation Recommendations for Rice

First Two Weeks:

  • Keep rice paddies flooded to promote germination and emergence of rice seeds.

  • Apply additional water as needed to prevent soil drying and ensure uniform seedling establishment.

Third Month:

  • Continue flooded conditions during panicle initiation and flowering stages to support pollination and grain filling.

  • Implement intermittent drying during late grain filling to promote grain maturity and reduce lodging risks.

Next 3 Months:

  • Gradually reduce water depth during ripening and maturation to facilitate grain drying and harvest readiness.
  • Drain rice paddies completely before harvest to allow for easier mechanical harvesting and reduce grain moisture content.
  • Implement proper water management practices post-harvest to prepare fields for subsequent crop rotation.

Soil Health Recommendations

{% if soil_type == 'DRY' %}

For Dry Soil:

  • Implement drip irrigation systems to conserve moisture.
  • Apply organic mulches like straw or wood chips to the soil surface to retain moisture and suppress weed growth.
  • Use drought-tolerant crop varieties such as sorghum or chickpeas adapted to arid conditions.
  • Practice minimal tillage or no-till farming to reduce soil disturbance and maintain soil moisture levels.
{% elif soil_type == 'HUMID' %}

For Humid Soil:

  • Monitor soil moisture levels regularly to prevent waterlogging and nutrient leaching.
  • Install proper drainage systems like tile drains or French drains to remove excess water from the soil profile.
  • Rotate crops with shallow-rooted species like lettuce or cabbage to prevent soil compaction and improve soil structure.
  • Apply organic matter such as compost or green manure to enhance soil fertility and improve water retention.
{% elif soil_type == 'WET' %}

For Wet Soil:

  • Improve soil drainage through land leveling or installing drainage ditches to remove excess water from the soil.
  • Choose crops adapted to waterlogged conditions such as rice, cranberries, or certain varieties of wheat and barley.
  • Avoid heavy machinery operation on wet soils to prevent compaction and soil degradation.
  • Incorporate organic matter like compost or peat moss to improve soil structure and water retention.
